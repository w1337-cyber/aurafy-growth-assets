TL;DR verdict

Take Profit Trader (TPT) — pick if you want a linear funded ladder and don't mind the trail mechanic changing under you between phases. 3-phase lifecycle: Test (EOD trail, 50% consistency) → PRO simulated funded (intraday trail, no consistency, 80/20 split) → PRO+ live-funded promotion (back to EOD, 90/10 split, auto-promotion since 2026-03-18). Tradeify — pick if you want a 1-step eval, a uniform EOD trail across the lifecycle, and you can stay off NinjaTrader. 3 parallel programs at signup: Growth (eval-free consistency → funded 35%), Select (eval 40% → funded-free), Lightning instant-funded (progressive 20% → 25% → 30%). EOD trail uniform across all three programs. Locks at SB + $100 — same family as Apex 4.0 / MFFU. Tradesea / Tradovate / WealthCharts only. $0 activation. News-trading explicitly permitted.
The lifecycle landmine is the story. Neither firm gives you one set of rules to learn. TPT shifts the trail mechanic temporally (per phase). Tradeify shifts the consistency rule structurally (per program you picked at signup). The lock family, platform stack, activation cost, and news-trading policy are all opposite too.

The lifecycle-landmine wedge: what shifts on you

The single most underrated quirk in any prop firm's rulebook is what changes between when you sign up and when you cash a payout. TPT and Tradeify both build a shift into the lifecycle — on totally different axes.

TPT — the trail mechanic shifts per phase

Test: EOD trail (drawdown floor only updates on the daily close). Pass the profit target, get auto-promoted to PRO. PRO: intraday trail (the floor advances on every new equity high during the session). Pass PRO and you're auto-promoted to PRO+ (since 2026-03-18). PRO+: back to EOD.

Tradeify — the consistency rule shifts per program

Growth: no consistency rule on eval — pass however you want — then 35% consistency on funded. Select: 40% consistency on eval — then no consistency rule on funded. Lightning: progressive 20% → 25% → 30% as you take payouts. The trail stays EOD across all three programs.

This matters because every shift is a place where a trader who learned one set of rules gets caught by the other. The two patterns target opposite types of trader:

  • TPT's temporal shift means your discipline strategy has to change between Test and PRO. A grinder who scales out into closes (smart on EOD) can give back trail headroom on a PRO intraday spike. The promotion is automatic — you can't opt out of the trail change.
  • Tradeify's structural shift means you pick your fate at signup. If you signed up for Select because the activation upgrade looked attractive, you have a 40% consistency wall on eval. If you signed up for Growth because it had no eval consistency, you face 35% on funded.
Neither shift is hidden — both are documented — but both are the single biggest source of "wait, the rule was different last week" confusion in each firm's community.

Lock archetype: same word, different family

Both firms use a "trailing drawdown that locks." The lock point is different and it materially changes the math.

TPT — locks at the Starting Balance

Trail floor stops moving once equity exceeds (Starting Balance + Drawdown). E.g. 100K with $3,000 drawdown: floor advances until equity hits $103,000, then it freezes at $100,000. Same archetype as Topstep funded.

Tradeify — locks at SB + $100

Trail floor stops moving once equity exceeds (Starting Balance + Drawdown + $100). E.g. 100K Growth with $3,500 drawdown: floor advances until equity hits $103,600, then it freezes at $100,100. Same archetype as Apex 4.0 / MFFU / Bulenox / Tradeify.

The $100 difference is not the point — the lock family is. TPT's funded ladder is structurally a Topstep cousin; Tradeify's is structurally an Apex cousin. That dictates which other rule patterns each firm tends to inherit (Tradeify's progressive Lightning consistency, for instance, is more Apex-shaped than Topstep-shaped).

Platforms: the biggest gotcha

If you defaulted into one of these firms because of brand recognition, check the platform list before paying for an account.

TPT supports:

NinjaTrader 8, Tradovate, TradingView, Rithmic. Most prop-firm-trader-default platforms covered.

Tradeify supports:

Tradesea (Rithmic data feed), Tradovate, WealthCharts. NinjaTrader, TradingView, and MetaTrader are NOT supported.

If you trade on NinjaTrader 8 or TradingView, Tradeify is off the table. This is the single most common surprise for traders comparing the two firms. TPT's stack is the wider one.

Payouts and total cost

TPT — $130 activation, daily payouts, splits step up

Activation fee is $130 per account, paid up-front when you convert from Test to PRO. Payouts on PRO/PRO+ are daily once your buffer above starting balance is cleared (7-day minimum between payouts; 1–2 business day processing). Split steps up from 80/20 on PRO to 90/10 on PRO+ (auto-promoted).

Tradeify — $0 baseline, 5-day default cadence, 90/10 from $15K

No activation fee on the base programs. Payouts are 5-day cadence by default. Select offers two optional upgrades — $1,500 one-time for daily payouts, or $4,000 for "flex" payouts. 60-minute guaranteed processing. Split is 90/10 on Simulated Funded after the first $15K of profits, 80/20 on Elite Live.

If you intend to scale to multiple accounts, TPT's per-account activation adds up faster (e.g. 5 accounts = $650 in activation). Tradeify's $0 baseline is friendlier for multi-account stacking — but if you want daily payouts on Select, the $1,500 upgrade is a real cost. Lightning has no activation but charges more for the eval ($345–$796 range vs Growth's $99–$369).

Worked examples

Example 1 — A 50K trader hits a +$1,200 day during the eval phase

Same eval-stage trader hits a single $1,200 winning day on a 50K account.

  • TPT Test (50K): Daily P&L counts toward the 50% consistency rule. To keep $1,200 within the 50% rule, the trader needs phase-total profit ≥ $2,400 by the time they request the payout / complete the phase. Profit target on Test 50K is $3,000 — one big day this size still leaves room to grind out the last $1,800 across multiple smaller sessions before requesting promotion.
  • Tradeify Growth (50K) eval: No consistency rule on Growth eval. The $1,200 day counts in full toward the $3,000 profit target. The trader can finish the entire profit target in two more sessions and request funded account creation as fast as the 1-day minimum allows.
  • Tradeify Select (50K) eval: 40% consistency rule. To keep $1,200 within the rule, phase-total profit ≥ $3,000 needed — exactly the target. Functionally similar to TPT Test at 50% but slightly tighter due to the 40% threshold.
A trader who likes to "spike-and-hold" days will be happiest on Tradeify Growth eval (no rule). A trader who runs steady-edge multi-day sessions can be on any of the three with no issue.

Example 2 — A 100K funded trader takes an intraday $2,800 winner that closes back at $1,400

Identical session: equity spikes to +$2,800 intraday, gives back $1,400, closes at +$1,400 over the day. Account is 100K with the standard drawdown for each firm's program.

  • TPT PRO (100K, $3,000 trail): PRO is the intraday trail phase. Floor advances on the wick: new floor = max(prior floor, 100,000 + 2,800 − 3,000) = $99,800. The wick raises the floor regardless of the close.
  • TPT PRO+ (100K, $3,000 trail): PRO+ reverts to EOD trail. Floor only updates on the daily close. New floor = 100,000 + 1,400 − 3,000 = $98,400. The wick is ignored. Same firm, opposite outcome — this is the TPT phase shift in one trade.
  • Tradeify Growth (100K, $3,500 trail): EOD across all programs. Floor = 100,000 + 1,400 − 3,500 = $97,900. The wick is ignored. Same logic on Select and Lightning.
The TPT PRO → PRO+ jump changes the same trade from "wick costs you trail headroom" to "wick is ignored." If you held all three accounts side-by-side — PRO, PRO+, and any Tradeify program — you'd see the firms agree on the outcome of this trade twice (PRO+ matches Tradeify) and disagree once (PRO matches nothing).

FAQ

Can I run a TPT account and a Tradeify account at the same time?

Yes — the two firms are independent. Both ban cross-account hedging within the same firm. Cross-firm hedging is not enforceable by either firm's rulebook (they can't see your other firm's positions), but it's still bad risk practice — do not coordinate equal-and-opposite positions between them.

If I pass TPT Test, can I refuse the PRO promotion to stay on EOD trail?

No. Promotion from Test to PRO is automatic when you clear the profit target. The same applies to PRO → PRO+ since the 2026-03-18 auto-promotion change — TPT promotes accounts based on internal consistency / risk / execution review. You can't opt out of the intraday-trail PRO phase if you want to be funded with TPT.

If I pick Tradeify Growth and regret it, can I switch to Select?

You can't convert an existing account — but Tradeify allows up to 5 active funded accounts. You can run one Growth, one Select, and one Lightning in parallel and discover which program matches your trading style without losing the existing account. TPT's 5-account cap is the same.

Which has a faster path to first payout?

Tradeify Lightning is instant-funded (no eval), so first-payout timing is gated only by the 5-day default cadence and the 20% first-payout consistency rule. TPT requires you to clear Test (5-day minimum) then start the PRO phase — daily payouts on PRO open as soon as the buffer above starting balance is cleared, with a 7-day minimum between payouts. Lightning wins on raw days-to-first-cash; TPT wins on lower upfront eval cost per dollar of funded capital.

Does either firm allow news trading?

Tradeify explicitly permits it. TPT bans news trading on PRO/PRO+ (no trading 1 minute before, during, or after FOMC, NFP, CPI). TPT Test has no news restriction. This is the single biggest behavioral-rule difference — if your edge relies on the open after CPI, Tradeify is the one to choose.

Does either firm allow EA / automated trading?

Both restrict pure HFT and copy-trading-across-your-own-accounts. Personal EAs that don't constitute HFT are tolerated case-by-case — the rulebook language is similar at both. Read each firm's current TOS before deploying a bot.

Is Tradeify's microscalping floor actually enforced?

The rule states more than 50% of your trades AND more than 50% of your profit must come from positions held longer than 10 seconds. The target is sub-second scalpers and tick-arb strategies. Standard intraday scalping (~30 sec holds and up) is fine. TPT has no equivalent rule.
