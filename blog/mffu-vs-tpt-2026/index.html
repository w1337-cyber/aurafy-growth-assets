Trail mechanics & the decision source

This is the structural wedge that defines MFFU vs TPT. Both firms expose the trader to multiple trail types. The question is: who chooses, and when?

MFFU — three trail types, trader-chosen at signup

Core (50K only) EOD trail. Updates once per day at session close. 40% consistency on eval AND funded. Lock at SB+$100 = $50,100. 3-day minimum.
Rapid (50K, 100K, 150K) INTRADAY trail — recomputes on every new intraday equity high. No consistency rule at all. Lock at SB+$100.
Pro (50K, 100K, 150K) EOD trail. No eval — instant-funded simulated account. $100K total payout cap across all Pro accounts you hold. Lock at SB+$100.

A trader picks the plan that matches their style at signup. Holds the rule shape for that account's life. Can open a second account on a different plan and run them in parallel — nothing about owning a Core account restricts you from also opening a Rapid or a Pro. The plans never interact within one account, but the trader's overall posture stays fluid.

TPT — three trail types, firm-imposed in series

Test (eval) EOD trail. Updates once per day at session close. 50% consistency. 5-day minimum. Pass at $3,000 profit on 50K.
PRO (simulated funded) INTRADAY trail — recomputes on every new intraday high. No consistency. 80/20 split. 1 trading day per calendar week minimum.
PRO+ (live funded) EOD trail (reverts). No consistency. 90/10 split. Real exchange routing. Auto-promoted since 2026-03-18.

A trader signs up for Test only — there is no other entry point at TPT. Pass Test, get moved to PRO. Pass PRO's internal review, get moved to PRO+. The trail TYPE itself changes between phases, firm-imposed. The trader does not choose; cannot opt out; cannot skip. The middle-phase intraday trail is the highest-confusion event in TPT's lifecycle, and the firm's own rulebook flags it.

The decision-source asymmetry: Both firms expose the trader to EOD and intraday trail mechanics. MFFU lets the trader pick which one to live under by which plan they buy. TPT makes the trader live under one, then the other, then the first again, in a fixed order. A trader who knows their style fits EOD can spend $0 activation at MFFU and buy a Core or Pro account, never touching an intraday trail. The same trader at TPT pays $130 for Test, passes on EOD, then has to re-validate the same style under an intraday PRO trail to keep the account funded.

Deep dive → Trailing drawdown across 7 prop firms — MFFU and TPT are the cluster's clearest example of "same mechanics, opposite decision source." Apex 4.0 makes the trader pick EOD or intraday at signup, permanent (similar to MFFU's plan-pick). Bulenox does the same with Option 1 vs Option 2. Earn2Trade locks every phase to EOD. TPT is the only firm in the dataset that mandates a trail-TYPE shift inside a single account's lifecycle.

Lock family: SB+$100 vs SB exactly

The lock floor is where MFFU and TPT split into different cluster archetypes. The $100 gap is small in dollar terms but defines two distinct lock-family camps across the seven-firm cluster.

MFFU — SB + $100 (Apex 4.0 family) Lock fixes $100 above starting balance once the trail unlocks. On 50K: floor at $50,100. On 100K: floor at $100,100. Same archetype as Apex 4.0, Bulenox, Tradeify. The $100 cushion functionally means a trader who pulls back to flat does not blow the account — only a small loss below SB triggers stop-out.
TPT — SB exactly (Topstep family) Lock fixes at starting balance once the trail unlocks. On 50K: floor at $50,000. On 100K: floor at $100,000. Same archetype as Topstep funded, Earn2Trade. No cushion — the trader who pulls back exactly to flat is at the floor. One tick below SB closes the account.
The honest impact of the $100 gap: Most traders never test the difference because they never run the account to the exact-flat boundary. The $100 matters in two specific situations: (a) a trader who hovers around break-even during a slump — at MFFU they have a tiny buffer, at TPT they're on the knife-edge; (b) a trader who passes the eval at the exact profit target and immediately wants to start withdrawing — at MFFU the funded account starts $100 above the trail floor, at TPT it starts AT the floor. In practice, the bigger consequence of the lock-family split is which cluster the firm rhymes with on every other rule, not the $100 itself.

Consistency: plan-variable vs phase-locked

Both firms apply consistency, but the structure is opposite. MFFU's consistency varies by which plan the trader picked at signup. TPT's consistency varies by which phase the firm has put the trader in.

MFFU — varies by plan, trader chose it Core: 40% on eval AND funded (single day's profit ≤ 40% of total). Rapid: none at all. Pro: N/A (no eval). The trader signs up knowing which consistency regime they live under for that account's life. Want zero consistency tax? Pick Rapid. Want consistency on funded too? Pick Core.
TPT — varies by phase, firm chose it Test: 50% (single day's profit ≤ 50% of total). PRO: none. PRO+: none. The trader cannot avoid the 50% rule — every TPT account starts at Test. Pass Test, the rule dies; on PRO and PRO+, no consistency at all.
The threshold and the scope: MFFU Core's 40% is stricter than TPT Test's 50% (lower threshold = tighter). But MFFU Core enforces it on FUNDED too — the only plan in the comparison where consistency persists post-pass. TPT's consistency dies entirely at the eval gate. A grinder who can hold a 40% ratio every payout cycle has a home at MFFU Core. A high-day-spread trader passing one big day then several small ones has a home at TPT Test (50% leaves more room for one dominant day) and at MFFU Rapid (no rule at all). Same threshold-region, opposite enforcement scope.

TPT, Topstep, Earn2Trade all drop consistency at the eval gate. Apex 4.0 enforces 30% on both eval AND funded forever. Bulenox 40% applies on Master+Funded but NOT during Qualification. Activation & cost model: the cleanest cost-of-attempt wedge

MFFU and TPT approach the cost of attempting from opposite directions on activation, even though they're structurally similar on the DLL question.

MFFU $0 upfront activation. Pay monthly subscription on Core or Rapid eval (price varies by size). On pass, no extra activation fee — the account transitions to funded with no separate cost. Reset available on the eval phase. Pro plan is a one-time purchase (instant funding, no eval, monthly-sub-free).
TPT $130 upfront per Test account. No monthly subscription. Pay the activation once per attempt, pass or fail. Pass Test, auto-promoted to PRO with no extra cost. Auto-promoted to PRO+ since 2026-03-18 with no extra cost. Test reset available; PRO+ reset NOT permitted — bust live, account closes permanently.
The economic asymmetry: A trader who passes their first attempt at TPT pays $130 total to get to a funded account. A trader who passes their first attempt at MFFU Core in 30 days pays roughly one month's subscription — price varies but in a comparable range. On a fast pass, both are cheap, with TPT slightly favored. A trader who takes six attempts at TPT pays $780 sunk regardless. A trader who grinds MFFU Core for six months pays 6× subscription — comparable total, but on a recurring rather than per-attempt structure. The bigger asymmetry: MFFU Pro lets the trader skip the eval entirely by paying one-time for an instant-funded simulated account; TPT has no instant-funded path.

MFFU Pro instant-funding vs TPT phase ladder

This is the structural divergence with no analogue on either side. MFFU has a "skip the eval" option that TPT does not.

  • MFFU Pro is sold as an instant-funded simulated account. Pay one-time, no eval phase, no profit target, no minimum trading days. The account is funded simulated on day one with an EOD trail, lock at SB+$100. The catch: $100K total payout cap across all Pro accounts the trader holds. Hit the cap, no more Pro account payouts (additional Core or Rapid accounts on other plans remain unaffected).
  • TPT has no instant-funded path. Every account starts at Test. A trader who knows they're past the eval-style learning curve has no shortcut at TPT — they pay $130, sit through a 5-day minimum on Test (even if they pass profit target sooner), then get moved to PRO and have to re-validate under an intraday trail. The fastest path to a real-capital payout is at least one Test pass.

For a trader who has already passed multiple evals at other firms and is confident in their style, MFFU Pro is the cluster's most efficient route to a funded simulated account. For a trader who values the validation of an evaluation gate and wants the live-capital step (PRO+ at TPT is real exchange routing, MFFU funded is simulated payouts only), TPT is structurally what they want.

TPT lifecycle landmines: (a) The trail TYPE shift from Test (EOD) to PRO (intraday) is the highest-confusion event — passed-fine-then-blown-after-promote is the documented failure mode. (b) PRO+ promotion is automatic and irrevocable in the sense that PRO+ resets are not permitted — busting the live account closes it permanently with no option to restart. (c) PRO and PRO+ ban news trading 1 minute before/during/after FOMC, NFP, CPI. (d) All positions must be flat by 5pm ET every day.
MFFU lifecycle landmines: (a) Core's 40% consistency persists on FUNDED — a trader who passes Core eval clean and then has one big payout-cycle day can fail the consistency check at the funded gate, not just eval. (b) Pro's $100K total payout cap is across ALL Pro accounts, not per account — opening a second Pro account does not double the cap. (c) Rapid's intraday trail means every new equity high recomputes the floor — the trader needs to be comfortable with that mechanic before signing up for Rapid (no chance to learn it on EOD first).

Worked scenario: the decision-source fork in action

A 50K-equivalent trader who already knows their style fits EOD execution and does not want to live under an intraday trail at any point. How does the lifecycle play out at each firm?

Scenario A — signed up for MFFU Core

  • Pays monthly subscription; $0 upfront activation. Trades the eval under EOD trail. Passes the eval in 8 sessions at +$3,200 profit. Single biggest day was $1,100 = 34% of total — cleared the 40% consistency rule.
  • Funded on Core. Trail mechanic does not change — same EOD trail as the eval phase. Lock at SB+$100 = $50,100. 40% consistency persists on funded payouts; the trader plans payouts to avoid any single day exceeding 40% of cycle profit.
  • Opens a second account on Pro for $X one-time. Now holds Core (proven, payout-cycled) and Pro (instant-funded, no eval) in parallel. The two accounts have independent equity curves but the trader has 2x funded simulated exposure with one rule shape (EOD trail) across both.
  • Outcome: zero exposure to intraday trail mechanics. The trader stayed inside their style preference for the account's entire life and was able to scale their funded exposure horizontally by buying another Pro account.

Scenario B — signed up for TPT Test

  • Pays $130 upfront activation for Test. Trades under EOD trail. Passes Test in 6 sessions at +$3,200 profit (had to wait 5 sessions minimum even though target was hit in 4). EOD trail floor sat at $50,000 the whole time because no day closed below SB.
  • Auto-promoted to PRO. Trail mechanic changes: now intraday HWM. Same trading style now has the floor recomputing every time the session prints a new equity high.
  • Week 3 of PRO: $1,400 intraday equity high at 11am, trader gives back to +$200 by close. The floor recomputed on the 11am high; the next morning a $1,600 intraday drawdown breaches the floor. Account closed.
  • Outcome: passed-Test-then-blown-PRO is the documented TPT failure mode. The trader didn't choose to live under intraday — the firm imposed it on a style that had only been validated on EOD. No "open another EOD account" escape hatch because every TPT account starts at Test, not at PRO+.
The structural insight: MFFU lets the trader's style preference dictate the rule shape for every account they open — permanently, with parallel-plan flexibility. TPT lets the firm's lifecycle design dictate the rule shape every account goes through — sequentially, with no opt-out. Same number of trail mechanics in play across each trader's experience; opposite source of who decides which one applies and when.

FAQ

Can I hold MFFU and TPT accounts at the same time?

Yes — the two firms are independent and cross-firm hedging is not enforceable by either rulebook (they can't see your other firm's positions). Both firms restrict cross-account coordination within their own platform — do not run equal-and-opposite positions across accounts at the same firm. Across firms, the accounts are practically independent. Many traders run MFFU Core for grinder style and a TPT Test for a separate breakout-style attempt.

Why does TPT change the trail type between phases?

TPT uses the trail-type shift as a phase-differentiator: EOD trail in Test (forgiving evaluation) → INTRADAY trail in PRO (tightens risk discipline at simulated funded) → EOD trail again in PRO+ (live-capital phase, reverts to the forgiving mechanic). The firm's logic is that PRO is the discipline-test phase between passing Test and earning real capital at PRO+. Whether that design serves traders is debatable — passed-Test-then-blown-PRO is the documented failure mode at TPT and is the single biggest source of post-eval regret. MFFU's plan-pick-at-signup avoids this by letting the trader live under one mechanic for an account's life.

Does MFFU have a daily loss limit on any plan?

No — MFFU has zero DLL on Core, Rapid, and Pro. The trailing drawdown is the only hard-loss guardrail across every plan. TPT also has no DLL since January 2025 firmwide. The two firms are the cluster's only no-DLL-on-every-plan archetype. Other firms in the cluster (Apex 4.0, Earn2Trade, Topstep, Bulenox Option 2) all apply some form of DLL.

What's the actual cost difference between MFFU Core and TPT Test on a 3-month grind?

MFFU Core: monthly subscription on the eval (varies by size, see the firm's current pricing) for up to 3 months until pass. TPT Test: $130 per attempt — 3 attempts over 3 months = $390. The structural difference: MFFU's cost stops on pass (you transition to funded with no extra activation), TPT's $130 is per-attempt and sunk regardless of outcome. On a fast first-attempt pass, TPT is competitive. On a multi-attempt grind, MFFU's per-month structure can come out cheaper if the trader settles into a stable pass rhythm.

What is MFFU Pro and how does it work?

MFFU Pro is an instant-funded simulated account. Pay one-time, no eval, no profit target, no minimum trading days. The account is funded simulated on day one with an EOD trail and lock at SB+$100. The catch: $100K total payout cap across all Pro accounts the trader holds. Hit the cap, no more Pro account payouts. Pro is the only path in this comparison that lets the trader skip the eval phase entirely — TPT has no analogue (every TPT account starts at Test). Pro suits traders who are confident in their style and want efficient access to a funded simulated account without a multi-week eval grind.

Is TPT PRO+ "real money" and is MFFU funded "real money"?

TPT PRO+ is live exchange routing — trades go to a real exchange, 90/10 profit split, real capital. Promotion from PRO to PRO+ is automatic since 2026-03-18. MFFU funded is simulated capital with real-dollar payouts — trades are not routed to a live exchange but the profits paid to the trader are real US dollars. Both produce real-money payouts; only TPT PRO+ also produces real-money executions. A trader for whom the "live trading" feel matters will care about this difference; a trader focused purely on payout dollars may not.

How does the consistency rule compare?

MFFU Core: 40% on eval AND funded (the only plan in the comparison that enforces consistency post-pass). MFFU Rapid: no consistency. MFFU Pro: no eval, no consistency. TPT Test: 50% on eval only, drops on PRO and PRO+. So a trader on MFFU Core lives under a stricter threshold (40%) for the account's entire life, while a trader at TPT lives under a looser threshold (50%) only during eval and gets dropped entirely after. The scope question (does the rule die at the eval gate?) matters more than the threshold for most traders — MFFU Core is the cluster's only plan where consistency taxes funded payouts every cycle.
