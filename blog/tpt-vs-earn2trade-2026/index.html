The wedge in plain English

Most prop-firm comparisons rank firms by drawdown size or profit target. TPT and Earn2Trade have basically identical numbers on the eval phase: 50K account, $2,000 trailing drawdown, $3,000 profit target, 6 mini contracts. Lock at $50,000 both. The structural story is about how many times the rule shape changes inside one account's life.

TPT shifts the rule shape twice, mandatorily. Three phases, three rule profiles: Test (EOD trail, 50% consistency, 5-day min), PRO (INTRADAY trail, no consistency, 1-day-per-week min, 80/20 split), PRO+ (EOD trail again, no consistency, 90/10 split, real capital). The trail TYPE itself changes between phases — EOD in evaluation, INTRADAY at simulated funded, then EOD again at live funded. This is the highest-confusion event in TPT's lifecycle and the firm's own rulebook flags it.

Earn2Trade shifts the rule shape once, optionally. Two states: Eval (EOD trail, 30% consistency, 10-day min) and Funded (EOD trail, no consistency, 80/20 split). The funded state has an optional sub-fork — stay on LiveSim simulated capital or convert to Live real capital — but the trail mechanic is identical on both sides of that fork. And 94.77% of 2025 passers chose to stay on LiveSim. So for nearly every trader, the rule shape changes exactly once (at the eval gate) and stays that way forever.

Same lock floor — opposite lifecycle volatility. That's the spine of this comparison.

Trail mechanics & the phase shift

This is the structural wedge that defines TPT vs Earn2Trade. The lock floor is identical (SB exactly on both). The trail type across the account's lifecycle is structurally inverted.

TPT — trail type shifts twice across the lifecycle

Test (eval) EOD trail. Updates once per day at session close. 50% consistency. 5-day minimum. Pass at $3,000 profit on 50K.
PRO (simulated funded) INTRADAY trail — updates on every new intraday high. No consistency. 80/20 split. 1 trading day per calendar week minimum.
PRO+ (live funded) EOD trail (reverts). No consistency. 90/10 split. Real capital. Auto-promoted since 2026-03-18.

The middle phase is the trap. A trader passes Test on an EOD trail (which forgives intraday wicks), then gets moved to PRO where the trail recomputes on every new intraday high — the same wick that didn't matter in Test now ratchets the floor up. This is the highest-confusion lifecycle event at TPT and the firm's rulebook flags it specifically.

Earn2Trade — one EOD trail, every phase

  • EOD-only trail across both products (Gauntlet Mini & Trader Career Path).
  • Same EOD mechanic in eval, on LiveSim, on Live. Trail updates once per day on the closing balance.
  • Locks at Starting Balance — same lock point as TPT.
  • No phase-shift surprise. What you traded under in eval is the same mechanic on every subsequent phase.
The mechanic that bites: TPT's PRO phase is structurally aggressive on intraday volatility. A trader whose Test phase looked clean on EOD — closing flat after some intraday dispersion — can find the PRO trail recomputing the floor higher every time their session wicks. Earn2Trade keeps the same trail mechanic forever, so a trader who passed eval on EOD doesn't have to re-validate their style under a different rule shape. If you've never traded under an intraday trail, TPT's middle phase is where that learning happens — on a live-payout simulated account where mistakes cost real money via lost payout cadence.

Consistency: same scope, different threshold

Both firms apply consistency on the eval phase only and drop it on the funded side. The threshold is what differs — and the threshold flips which trader-type each firm rewards.

TPT — 50% on Test, dies post-Test Single day's profit ÷ total profit ≤ 0.50 at the point of passing. Loosest threshold in the cluster — lets a single day be up to half of total profit. On a $3,000 pass target, max single day = $1,500. Drops entirely on PRO and PRO+.
Earn2Trade — 30% on eval, dies on LiveSim Single day's profit ÷ total profit ≤ 0.30 at the moment of passing. Tighter than TPT and same threshold as Apex 4.0. On a $3,000 pass target, max single day = $900. Drops entirely on LiveSim and Live.
The honest take: TPT's 50% loose-eval consistency is friendly to high-day-spread traders — the trader who has one big day plus several small days. Earn2Trade's 30% rewards the steady-grinder profile — consistent smaller days, no single dominant day. Both firms drop the rule entirely once funded, so the consistency question is purely a pass-the-eval question, not a lifetime tax. Pick the threshold that fits how you actually pass evaluations.

Daily loss limit: present vs absent

The DLL axis is where TPT and Earn2Trade are most structurally opposite. One firm removed the DLL entirely in 2025; the other applies a strict DLL at every account size.

TPT — no daily loss limit since January 2025 Removed firmwide. The trailing drawdown is the only hard-loss guardrail. A trader can give back any amount intraday without triggering a session lockout — until they hit the trail floor. Maximum freedom on daily risk; maximum exposure to a single bad session.
Earn2Trade — DLL on every account size GAU50 / TCP50: $1,100 daily limit. GAU100 / TCP100: $2,200. GAU150: $3,300. GAU200: $4,400. TCP25: $550. Hitting the limit closes the day. A built-in session circuit-breaker that protects traders from themselves on a tilt day.
The risk-floor inversion: Most traders assume "no DLL" means TPT is the looser firm and Earn2Trade is the stricter. The honest read is the opposite for tilt-prone traders: Earn2Trade's DLL forces a hard stop before the trail floor is even at risk. TPT gives the trader nothing between "fine" and "blown trail" — one bad afternoon and the account is dead. A traders who has never been stopped out by a DLL might prefer TPT's freedom; a trader who has ever blown a Topstep on a single tilt session might prefer the Earn2Trade guardrail.

Activation model: the cleanest cost-of-attempt wedge

TPT and Earn2Trade approach the cost of attempting from opposite directions. TPT is one-time-fee, no subscription. Earn2Trade is subscription-only, fee deducted from first withdrawal.

TPT $130 upfront activation per Test account — that's it. No monthly subscription. Pass the Test, you're funded on PRO. Auto-promote to PRO+ (live capital) without an extra fee. Total cost-to-attempt = $130 per Test attempt, capped at the number of attempts you buy. PRO+ reset NOT permitted — bust the live account, it closes permanently.
Earn2Trade Monthly subscription only ($150–$550 depending on product/size). The $139 activation is deducted from your first withdrawal. If you never pass, you never pay activation. Total cost = monthly subscription × months until pass or quit. TCP includes 1 free reset per monthly rebill; GAU has no free resets.
The economic asymmetry: A trader who passes TPT on their first attempt pays $130 and is done with up-front cost. A trader who passes Earn2Trade in three months pays roughly $450–$550 (3 × subscription) and then $139 from first withdrawal. So on a fast pass, TPT is cheaper. A trader who takes six attempts at TPT pays $780; a trader who grinds Earn2Trade for six months pays $900–$1,650 + $139. On a slow grind, the answer flips depending on subscription tier. The bigger asymmetry is risk: TPT's $130 is sunk on every attempt regardless of outcome; Earn2Trade's $139 is contingent on actually winning.

LiveSim vs PRO+: the funded gate

Both firms have a "real capital" step beyond simulated funded — but the mandatory-vs-optional structure is opposite.

  • TPT PRO → PRO+ is automatic since 2026-03-18. No application, no extra cost. TPT reviews PRO accounts on internal consistency / risk / execution metrics and promotes qualifying accounts to PRO+ (live capital, 90/10 split, EOD trail). The 80/20 PRO split applies first, then the 90/10 PRO+ split once promoted. PRO+ reset is NOT permitted — bust the live account, it closes permanently.
  • Earn2Trade LiveSim → Live is optional. After passing eval, the trader is funded on simulated capital with a real-dollar 80% profit share paid weekly. The trader can convert to Live capital at any time at no penalty, OR stay on LiveSim forever. Same 80/20 split, same payouts on both sides.

The remarkable Earn2Trade data point: 94.77% of 2025 passers stayed on LiveSim. Only 5.23% converted to Live. Withdrawal participation was roughly equal across both groups. The funded gate is structurally optional and the data says nearly everyone chooses the simulated side.

TPT lifecycle landmines: (a) The trail TYPE shift from Test (EOD) to PRO (intraday) is the highest-confusion event — passed-fine-then-blown-after-promote is the documented failure mode. (b) PRO+ promotion is automatic and irrevocable in the sense that PRO+ resets are not permitted — busting the live account closes it permanently with no option to restart. (c) PRO and PRO+ ban news trading 1 minute before/during/after FOMC, NFP, CPI — a trader who built a style around news momentum will lose that style at the funded gate. (d) All positions must be flat by 5pm ET every day — no overnight on any phase.

Worked scenario: the regret-risk fork in action

A 50K account, EOD-style trader who passes the eval comfortably but has never traded under an intraday trail. How does the lifecycle play out at each firm?

Scenario A — signed up for TPT Test

  • Passes Test in 8 sessions at +$3,200 profit. EOD trail floor sat at $50,000 the whole time because no day closed below SB. Easy pass.
  • Auto-promoted to PRO. Trail mechanic changes: now intraday HWM. Same trading style now has the floor recomputing every time the session prints a new equity high.
  • Week 3 of PRO: $1,400 intraday equity high at 11am, trader gives back to +$200 by close. The floor recomputed on the 11am high; the next morning a $1,600 intraday drawdown breaches the floor. Account closed.
  • Outcome: passed-Test-then-blown-PRO is the documented TPT failure mode. The trader didn't learn anything new about their style — the rule shape changed under them.

Scenario B — signed up for Earn2Trade GAU50

  • Passes eval in 12 sessions at +$3,100 profit. EOD trail across the entire phase. 30% consistency cleared with no single day above $900.
  • Funded on LiveSim. Trail mechanic does not change — same EOD trail as the eval phase. Same DLL on each account size. The only thing that changes is the consistency rule dies.
  • Week 3 of LiveSim: same trading style, same trail mechanic. A $1,400 intraday equity high at 11am that gives back to +$200 by close has zero effect on the trail floor — only the closing balance moves it.
  • Outcome: zero regret-risk on the trail mechanic transition. The trader keeps trading exactly the style that passed the eval.
The structural insight: TPT's regret-risk lives at the funded gate — the rule shape itself changes under your feet. Earn2Trade's regret-risk lives at the eval gate — once past it, the rule shape stabilizes forever. Same lock floor; opposite lifecycle volatility profile.

FAQ

Why does TPT change the trail type between phases?

TPT uses the trail-type shift as a phase-differentiator: EOD trail in Test (forgiving evaluation) → INTRADAY trail in PRO (tightens risk discipline at simulated funded) → EOD trail again in PRO+ (live-capital phase, reverts to the forgiving mechanic). The firm's logic is that PRO is the discipline-test phase between passing Test and earning real capital at PRO+. Whether that design serves traders is debatable — passed-Test-then-blown-PRO is the documented failure mode at TPT and is the single biggest source of post-eval regret.

Why doesn't TPT have a daily loss limit?

TPT removed the daily loss limit firmwide in January 2025. The official rationale is that the trailing drawdown alone is sufficient as a hard-loss guardrail and that a DLL was creating session-lockout friction without changing aggregate firm risk. The practical effect for traders: there is no session circuit-breaker. A trader can give back any amount intraday without locking the session, until they hit the trail floor. Earn2Trade kept its DLL on every account size ($550–$4,400 by size) explicitly as a tilt-day guardrail.

What's the actual cost difference between TPT and Earn2Trade on a 3-month grind?

TPT: $130 per Test attempt, no monthly subscription. Three Test attempts over three months = $390. Earn2Trade GAU50: $170/month subscription for three months = $510, plus $139 deducted from first withdrawal if and only if you pass. So a 3-month, multi-attempt TPT grind costs roughly the same as a single Earn2Trade subscription run that pays out. The risk difference: TPT's $130 is sunk on every attempt regardless of pass/fail; Earn2Trade's $139 only hits if you actually win.

Is the Earn2Trade LiveSim "real money"?

The trading is on simulated capital — meaning your fills are not routed to a live exchange and Earn2Trade hedges or absorbs the trades on its own book. The payouts are real money. 80% of LiveSim profits in real US dollars, paid weekly with a $100 minimum. The 94.77% of 2025 passers who stayed on LiveSim treat it as their permanent funded account.

Is TPT PRO+ "real money"?

Yes — PRO+ is live capital trading, 90/10 split, EOD trail, real exchange routing. Promotion from PRO to PRO+ is automatic since 2026-03-18 (no application or extra fee) based on TPT's internal review of consistency / risk / execution metrics on the PRO account. PRO+ reset is NOT permitted — bust the live account and it closes permanently, no option to restart that specific account.

Can I run a TPT account and an Earn2Trade account at the same time?

Yes — the two firms are independent and cross-firm hedging is not enforceable by either rulebook (they can't see your other firm's positions). Both firms restrict cross-account activity within their own platform — Earn2Trade prohibits copy-trading across accounts; TPT applies similar restrictions on coordinated multi-account activity. Run accounts at both firms independently; do not coordinate equal-and-opposite positions between them.

How does the consistency rule differ in practice?

Same scope (eval-only, drops on funded), different threshold (TPT 50% vs Earn2Trade 30%). On a $3,000 pass target: TPT max single day = $1,500; Earn2Trade max single day = $900. TPT rewards a higher-variance pass — one big day plus support days. Earn2Trade rewards a steady-grind pass — multiple smaller days, no single dominant day. Topstep's 50% eval-only matches TPT's threshold; Apex 4.0's 30% on both phases matches Earn2Trade's threshold but applies forever (no funded-side drop). TPT and Earn2Trade are the cleanest cluster examples of "consistency dies at the funded gate," both with that same archetype.
