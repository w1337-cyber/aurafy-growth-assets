What Tradovate's built-in journal misses

Tradovate is a great execution platform — fast DOM, web-native, $0.25/contract default for prop accounts, and it's the platform Topstep, Apex, MyFundedFutures, and most futures prop firms route through. But the "Notes" and "Trade History" tabs are a flat list of fills, not a journal:

  • No chart context. You can see ES filled at 5824.25 @ 09:42. You cannot see where on the 5-minute chart that fill happened, what swing high it broke through, or what your stop looked like relative to the structure.
  • No R-multiples, no expectancy. Tradovate shows P&L in dollars per trade. It does not compute average R, win rate by setup, or expectancy by time-of-day — the metrics that tell you which strategy is actually compounding.
  • No prop-firm rule tracking. If you're on a Topstep XFA or Apex 4.0 funded account, the journal won't tell you how close you are to the trail-DD floor, whether today's P&L violates the 50% / 30% consistency rule, or how many losses in a row you can take before hitting the daily loss limit.
  • No screen recording. Tradovate captures your trades. It does not capture you trading — the news pop you ignored, the hesitation before entry, the revenge add that turned a -1R into -3R. That's where the behavioral edge lives, and Tradovate's journal silently throws it away.
  • No playbook / setup tagging. You cannot tag a fill as "VWAP reclaim" or "ICT NY breakout" and pull a stats table for that setup alone.
The honest framing: Tradovate is the broker. A journal is a separate tool. Asking the broker to be both is like asking a debit card to be a budgeting app — it sees the transactions, but it doesn't help you make better ones.

~85% of Tradovate's active retail accounts are prop-firm funded or eval accounts (Tradovate is the default Topstep/Apex/MFFU platform). That's the audience this journal is built for. Aurafy tracks:
